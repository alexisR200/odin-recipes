Lomo Saltado

Description:

The Lomo Saltado is a popular Peruvian Chinese-Peruvian fusion dish,characterized by pieces of beef (lomo) sauteed with onion,tomato and yellow chili, traditionally served with French fries and rice.

Indredients:

  • 1 Pound of beef tenderloin, thinly sliced.
  • 2 Garlic cloves minced,Salt to taste,Pepper to taste.
  • 1 Cup of regular long grain white rice, uncooked.
  • 2 Cups of frozen french fries.
  • 3 Tablespoons of vegetable oil.
  • 1 Small red onion, thickly sliced.
  • 2 Roma tomatoes, thickly sliced.
  • 2 Teaspoons of yellow chili paste.
  • 2 Tablespoons of soy sauce.
  • 3 Tablespoons red wine vinegar.
  • 1/3 Cup beef broth.
  • 1/2 Cup chopped cilantro.

Steps:

  1. Marinate the meat: In a bowl, mix the strips of sirloin with the chopped garlic, salt, pepper, and cumin. Let it marinate for 10 to 15 minutes so the meat absorbs the flavors.
  2. Fry the potatoes: Prepare the fries. You can cut them into sticks and fry them until golden brown and crispy. Set them aside on a plate lined with paper towels.
  3. Stir-fry the meat: Heat a wok or large skillet over very high heat with a little oil. When it's very hot, add the marinated meat in small batches so it doesn't steam. Sear the meat on all sides for 1-2 minutes. Remove and set aside.
  4. Sauté the vegetables: In the same wok with the meat juices, add a little more oil if necessary. Sauté the onion and yellow chili (if using) until the onion becomes translucent and the edges are golden, but still crispy.
  5. Add the tomato and sauce: Add the tomato and cook for 1-2 more minutes. Then, add the soy sauce, vinegar, and yellow chili paste. Cook until the sauce evaporates and caramelizes slightly.
  6. Combine all ingredients: Return the meat to the wok and mix everything quickly. At this point, you can add a splash of pisco to sauté if you like, although this is optional.
  7. Finish the dish: Add the chips and chopped cilantro. Mix one last time to combine the ingredients, making sure not to overcook them so they don't lose their texture.
  8. Serving: Serve the lomo saltado immediately, accompanied by cooked white rice. The dish should be piping hot to enjoy its freshness and juiciness.
